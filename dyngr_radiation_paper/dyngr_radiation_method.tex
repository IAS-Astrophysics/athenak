\section{Scope}

This note documents the standalone \texttt{dyn\_radiation} solver.  The solver
is intentionally separate from the existing \texttt{radiation} module.  The
legacy module assumes a stationary Cartesian Kerr-Schild (CKS) metric and stores
the covariant Killing-energy form of the intensity.  The new module has a CKS
compatibility mode and an ADM mode intended for dynamical metric backgrounds
such as the superposed boosted Kerr-Schild binary in \texttt{dynbbh.cpp}.

The first ADM implementation is deliberately conservative:
\begin{itemize}
  \item spatial transport uses ADM Valencia coordinate speeds,
  \item the evolved variable is the Eulerian densitized intensity,
  \item an explicit ADM geometric energy source reproduces the Valencia energy
        source after summing over angles,
  \item full angular geodesic bending on a time-dependent ADM metric is not yet
        enabled, so ADM mode requires \texttt{angular\_fluxes=false}.
\end{itemize}

\section{Legacy Stationary-Metric Solver}

The existing solver discretizes the grey specific intensity on angular bins
\(n\) of a geodesic sphere.  In a local orthonormal tetrad,
\begin{equation}
  q^{(\hat{\alpha})}_n = (1,\ell_n,m_n,p_n),\qquad
  \ell_n^2+m_n^2+p_n^2=1 .
\end{equation}
The analytic CKS tetrad \(e_{(\hat{\alpha})}^{\ \mu}\) gives the coordinate
null direction
\begin{equation}
  k^\mu_n = e_{(\hat{\alpha})}^{\ \mu} q^{(\hat{\alpha})}_n,\qquad
  k_{\mu,n}=g_{\mu\nu}k^\nu_n .
\end{equation}
For the stationary metric, the conserved variable is
\begin{equation}
  U_n = k^0_n k_{0,n} \I_n .
  \label{eq:legacyU}
\end{equation}
The primitive reconstructed for spatial fluxes is \(k_{0,n}\I_n=U_n/k^0_n\),
and the face flux in direction \(i\) is
\begin{equation}
  F^i_n = k^i_n k_{0,n}\I_n .
\end{equation}
In grey form, the finite-volume update is
\begin{equation}
  \partial_t U_n + \partial_i F^i_n + \nabla_\Omega\cdot A_n = S_n ,
\end{equation}
where \(A_n\) is the angular flux generated by Ricci rotation coefficients.
Because the CKS metric and tetrad are stationary, the tetrad, face direction
maps, and angular drift coefficients are precomputed once.

\section{ADM Radiation Variables}

In ADM variables,
\begin{equation}
  \dd s^2=-\alpha^2\dd t^2
  +\gamma_{ij}(\dd x^i+\beta^i\dd t)(\dd x^j+\beta^j\dd t),
\end{equation}
with Eulerian normal
\begin{equation}
  n^\mu=\left(\frac{1}{\alpha},-\frac{\beta^i}{\alpha}\right).
\end{equation}
The ADM radiation tetrad uses the Eulerian observer as its timelike leg:
\begin{equation}
  e_{(\hat{0})}^{\ \mu}=n^\mu,\qquad
  e_{(\hat{a})}^{\ \mu}=(0,E_{(\hat{a})}^{\ i}),\qquad
  \gamma_{ij}E_{(\hat{a})}^{\ i}E_{(\hat{b})}^{\ j}=\delta_{\hat{a}\hat{b}} .
\end{equation}
For an angular-bin direction \(\ell_n^{(\hat{a})}\), define
\begin{equation}
  s^i_n = E_{(\hat{a})}^{\ i}\ell_n^{(\hat{a})},\qquad
  \gamma_{ij}s^i_ns^j_n=1 .
\end{equation}
Photons move through the grid with coordinate velocity
\begin{equation}
  v^i_n=\frac{\dd x^i}{\dd t}=\alpha s^i_n-\beta^i .
  \label{eq:admspeed}
\end{equation}
The ADM solver evolves
\begin{equation}
  U_n = \sqrt{\gamma}\,\I_n ,
  \label{eq:admU}
\end{equation}
with spatial flux
\begin{equation}
  F^i_n=\sqrt{\gamma}\,v^i_n\I_n .
  \label{eq:admflux}
\end{equation}
In flat space, \(\sqrt{\gamma}=1\), \(\alpha=1\), and \(\beta^i=0\), so
Equations~\eqref{eq:admU}--\eqref{eq:admflux} reduce to ordinary transport of
the tetrad-frame intensity.

\section{Valencia Moment Consistency}

The Eulerian radiation moments represented by the angular solution are
\begin{align}
  E &= \sum_n w_n \I_n,\\
  F^i &= \sum_n w_n \I_n s^i_n,\\
  P^{ij} &= \sum_n w_n \I_n s^i_n s^j_n ,
\end{align}
where \(w_n\) is the angular quadrature weight.  The Valencia energy equation in
the absence of matter coupling is
\begin{equation}
  \partial_t(\sqrt{\gamma}E)
  +\partial_i\left[\sqrt{\gamma}(\alpha F^i-\beta^iE)\right]
  =\sqrt{\gamma}\left(\alpha P^{ij}K_{ij}-F^i\partial_i\alpha\right).
  \label{eq:valenciaE}
\end{equation}
The per-angle source used in \texttt{dyn\_radiation} is
\begin{equation}
  S^{\mathrm{geom}}_n
  = U_n\left(\alpha K_{ij}s^i_ns^j_n-s^i_n\partial_i\alpha\right).
  \label{eq:anglesource}
\end{equation}
Summing Equation~\eqref{eq:anglesource} over angles gives exactly the right-hand
side of Equation~\eqref{eq:valenciaE}.  This is the main reason the ADM branch
does not need explicit time derivatives of gauge fields: the normal-time metric
evolution is represented by \(K_{ij}\), as in Valencia GRMHD.

The corresponding momentum equation is
\begin{equation}
  \partial_t(\sqrt{\gamma}S_j)
  +\partial_i\left[\sqrt{\gamma}(\alpha P^i_{\ j}-\beta^iS_j)\right]
  =\sqrt{\gamma}\left[
    \frac{1}{2}\alpha P^{ik}\partial_j\gamma_{ik}
    +S_i\partial_j\beta^i
    -E\partial_j\alpha\right].
\end{equation}
The present ADM transport does not yet include the angular drift operator needed
to reproduce this momentum source at the discrete angular level.  This is why
\texttt{angular\_fluxes=false} is enforced for ADM mode.

\section{Algorithm}

At each explicit stage, ADM mode performs:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item update or fill ADM variables;
  \item construct the Eulerian tetrad by a Cholesky factorization of
        \(\gamma_{ij}\);
  \item fill cell-centered \(\sqrt{\gamma}\) and face-centered
        \(\sqrt{\gamma}\), \(\alpha\), \(\beta^i\), and face tetrad columns;
  \item reconstruct \(\I_n=U_n/\sqrt{\gamma}\) in each spatial direction;
  \item compute face fluxes with \(v^i_n=\alpha s^i_n-\beta^i\);
  \item update \(U_n\) with flux divergences;
  \item apply Equation~\eqref{eq:anglesource};
  \item apply radiation source terms, boundary exchange, and AMR operations.
\end{enumerate}
The CKS compatibility path in \texttt{dyn\_radiation} keeps the legacy
normalization in Equation~\eqref{eq:legacyU}; this lets the old and new solvers
be compared directly on fixed Kerr-Schild backgrounds.

\section{Radiation Source and Output Normalizations}

The beam source uses the active radiation tetrad to build the local metric and
the beam angle.  It then writes the appropriate conserved variable:
\begin{equation}
  \Delta U_n =
  \begin{cases}
    k^0_n k_{0,n}\,\dot{\I}\,\Delta t, & \text{CKS/legacy normalization},\\
    \sqrt{\gamma}\,\dot{\I}\,\Delta t, & \text{ADM normalization}.
  \end{cases}
\end{equation}
Radiation-moment output similarly reconstructs
\begin{equation}
  \I_n =
  \begin{cases}
    U_n/(k^0_n k_{0,n}), & \text{CKS/legacy normalization},\\
    U_n/\sqrt{\gamma}, & \text{ADM normalization}.
  \end{cases}
\end{equation}
This distinction is necessary for the flat ADM beam test to match the flat CKS
beam test component by component.

\section{Null Particle Beam-Edge Tracers}

The particle module now has an opt-in \texttt{null\_geodesic} pusher.  Particle
real data are interpreted as
\begin{equation}
  (x,y,z,v_x,v_y,v_z)\quad\rightarrow\quad (x^i,k_i),
\end{equation}
where \(k_i\) is the covariant spatial photon momentum.  The coordinate-time
Hamiltonian is
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{H}(x^i,k_i)=\alpha\sqrt{\gamma^{ij}k_i k_j}-\beta^ik_i .
\end{equation}
The pusher advances
\begin{align}
  \frac{\dd x^i}{\dd t}
    &= \frac{\partial\mathcal{H}}{\partial k_i}
     = \alpha\frac{\gamma^{ij}k_j}{K}-\beta^i,\\
  \frac{\dd k_i}{\dd t}
    &= -\frac{\partial\mathcal{H}}{\partial x^i}
     = -(\partial_i\alpha)K
       -\frac{\alpha}{2K}(\partial_i\gamma^{jk})k_jk_k
       +(\partial_i\beta^j)k_j,
\end{align}
with \(K=\sqrt{\gamma^{ij}k_i k_j}\).  The current implementation uses the
nearest cell's ADM fields and centered finite differences.  This is adequate for
beam-edge diagnostics; higher-order interpolation can be added later without
changing the data layout.
